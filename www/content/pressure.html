PHYS1171 - Pressure

Pressure

Pressure is simply a measure of the force per unit area

$P=\frac{F}{A}$

Where P is the pressure, and F is the force applied normal to the area A. Pressure is a scalar quantity. The SI unit of pressure is the pascal, which has the symbol Pa. One pascal corresponds to a pressure of one newton of force per square meter. To put this number in perspective, normal atmospheric pressure is around 100 kPa at seal level.
